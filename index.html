05 Interview Experience

MathWorks — Why I Did Not Clear

Learning Experience

What Happened

My MathWorks interview was a turning point. I knew the concepts but couldn't translate them fast enough under pressure. The DSA rounds required edge-case awareness and optimized thinking within tight time limits. I had the knowledge but lacked the speed and structured communication to present solutions clearly.

Where I Fell Short

  • Under-invested in timed practice for edge-case heavy DSA problems
  • Couldn't articulate my thought process concisely during the interview
  • Spent too long on brute-force approaches before optimizing
  • Didn't practice enough mock interviews with real-time feedback

What Changed After

  • Weekly mock interviews with peers — explaining solutions out loud
  • Timed problem-solving sessions (25-min Pomodoro blocks)
  • Short revision notes for core CS fundamentals
  • Benchmarking progress with timed question sets on LeetCode
  • Focused on communication: "Think aloud, structure first, code second"

Key Takeaway

Understanding concepts is necessary but not sufficient. Speed, clarity under pressure, and the ability to communicate your thinking in real-time are equally critical. Every rejection is data — use it to calibrate your preparation strategy.
